SHREE MAHALINGESHWARA TEMPLE

The Puttur Mahalingeshwara Temple is an ancient temple which was constructed in the 12th century A.D. The temple is located in Puttur in the Dakshina Kannada district of Karnataka. The temple is currently maintained and conserved by the Directorate of Archaeology and Museums, under the Mysore division of the Government of Karnataka. This Temple is dedicated to an incarnation of Lord Shiva known as Lord Mahalingeshwara. On the western side of the temple is a pond. People believe that in ancient times, pearls were found in this pond. The pond has stone steps which can lead one to the water. The word Muttu in Kannada means pearl. This brought the name Muttur which later became Puttur

BIRUMALE HILL

Birumale Gudde (hills) is just 2 km away from the Puttur bus stand.Auto rickshaw are available to this place. Gudde means Hills in Tulu language. Gudde is the highest peak of Puttur. History says that there were tigers and wildlife before 60-70 years. But now it is a plain hill with lot of wind trees. The open stage of this hill is the centre of attraction for many street plays. This open stage is called the “Gandhi Mantapa”. There were many plays and cultural events performed here.

SHREE SAHASRALINGESHWARA TEMPLE UPPINANGADI

Sahasralingeshwara Temple at Uppinangady in Karnataka is dedicated to Shiva. The place is famous for thousands of lingas in the nearby river. The temple is believed to very old; the original Shivling of the Uppinangady Temple is covered by sand and is on the banks of Netravathi River – to the left of the temple. Worship is offered by removing the sand during annual temple festival. Shree Sahasralingeshwara and Mahakali temple in Uppinangady situated on the banks of holy rivers Nethravathi and Kumaradhara which meet and is called Sangama hence its called Sangama kshethra. The temple is well known as Dakshina Kashi where rituals to the souls of ancestors is performed.

SHREE KSHETRA GEJJEGIRI NANDAN BITHL

Shree kshetra Gejjegiri is a garody temple in Badagannur, Puttur. The temple is devoted to tulunad legendary twin hero’s koti chennaya. The story of these heroes may be taken to roughly five hundred years back, when reference to Ballads were made in the Tulu Padana. Koti and Chennayya were born to the Deyi Baidethi of the Daivashakthi people of Tulu Nadu. Owing to the brothers heroic deeds, they are worshipped and remembered as protectors.It is a Beautiful well maintained place.

BENDRU THEERTHA

Bendru Theertha is a natural hot water spring, located at a distance of about 15 kilometres from Puttur town in the Dakshina Kannada district of Karnataka, India, which is situated in an area near the river Seeresude, a small tributary of the River Chandragiri. Bendru Theertha contains traces of sulphur and is known to have curative properties. It is listed by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) as the only natural hot spring of South India. The temperature of the spring water generally remains at 99 F to 106 F.
